My Story

130+ Years of Bourgeois Grit

It started in 1891 at the Bourgeois Meat Market in Thibodaux, Louisiana. My family didn't just work. They built. Generation after generation, we put our names on things and then put the work behind them to make those names mean something.

That heritage was already in my blood when I raised my right hand in 2000 and joined the United States Marine Corps. I spent 12 years in uniform, including combat tours in Iraq and Afghanistan, learning what leadership actually looks like when the stakes aren't quarterly earnings but human lives.

"The military didn't teach me to follow orders. It taught me to make decisions when there are no good options and still move forward."

In 2010, my brother Nicholas Bourgeois, a Marine Corps Infantry Officer, was killed in action. That loss redefined everything. It stripped away the trivial. It made urgency permanent. Nicholas didn't get a tomorrow, and that reality burns through every decision I make. I don't waste time. I don't play small. And I don't tolerate excuses, from myself or anyone I work with.

After separating from the Marines in 2012, I bought my first Signal Security franchise in Baton Rouge. Within years, I scaled to 8 locations across Louisiana, Texas, and Mississippi, employing over 130 people. I learned every brutal lesson about hiring, firing, cash flow, partnerships, and the loneliness of ownership the hard way. No silver spoons. No safety nets.

In 2024, I launched The Bourgeois Strategy to take everything I'd learned in combat, in the boardroom, in failure, and in growth, and put it to work for other business owners. Not the ones looking for motivational quotes. The ones looking for someone who's actually been in the arena.

Outside of business, I'm a father of four. I pursue comedy because laughter keeps you human. I drum because rhythm demands presence. I model because confidence is a muscle. I'm a man of faith, reborn in 1995 and still being refined.

I'm not polished. I'm not a guru. I'm a work in progress who happens to have 130+ years of family entrepreneurial heritage, 12 years of military service, and 13 years of building businesses to draw from. If that's the kind of person you want in your corner, let's talk.
